🙌 Welcome inclusion

Make sure silent voices are heard. Help each member to find their mission and role during the jam. Every team member should make a meaningful contribution to the prototype. Also see the participation guidelines on inclusion, which contain the helpful "Rule of 1" and "Rule of n".

👍 Encourage positivity

Say "Yes, and ..." during brainstorming. Help others do the same.

💋 Keep It Simple, Silly

Focus on the most simple prototype first to test an idea. Extra features can always be added later. Instead of baking an entire wedding cake, start with a cupcake.

👣 Break it down

Take a complex idea and break it down into smaller steps. Help teams to get clear on what is happening. A simple Kanban board can work magic.

⏳ Watch the time

Please help teams to respect the schedule so we can stay on time. Sometimes brainstorms end up going in circles. As an outsider, you can spot patterns and offer interventions. A great timesaver for gathering and picking ideas is Note & Vote (essentially a single diamond, supercharged).

🖐 Get involved

Join in as much as you want: if you want to help a team with code or design, good for them. If you prefer to spread your advice over different teams, also great!

🎉 Have fun!

This Story Jam is a short retreat from reality: let’s make it weird and wonderful! Crazy ideas are welcome!

🔍 Identify needs

When you check in with a team, listen carefully to what they (actually) need. If you can offer this, please stick around. Otherwise please bring in another mentor or talk to the facilitators.

🔥 If you need help

… with anything, reach out to the facilitators. We are here to support you!

♦️♦️ Double the Diamond

All models are wrong, but some are useful. One model we've found particularly useful for co-creation during Story Jams, is the Double Diamond model, with its flows of divergent and convergent processes, pioneered by the UK Design Council.
